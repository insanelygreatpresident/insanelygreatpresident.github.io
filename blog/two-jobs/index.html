Two Jobs

Mar 17th, 2015

It is now a couple months later and I have been plugging through (a) reading about great presidents and leaders, (b) reading the memoirs of military fighters, © trying to figure out how to fund this presidency thing, and (d) trying to sell the book before I write it.

But along with the progress (or at least efforts) above, Rebecca and I have been having a ‘discussion’. You might call it a fight, but it is both more respectful and less animated than a fight normally is. The simple summary is that Rebecca says I can’t be president. Not that I am unelectable or unskilled at the job, but that simply she will not support me being president. For two months we have been discussing this.

I love and respect Rebecca, but I deal with all the financial needs of our family, so if I need to get a job to make ends meet, she can’t rightfully ‘veto’ that. She would have to first make the money I am making, and then she could convince me that a particular job wasn’t the best use of my time. My first reaction to her lack of support was to treat it as ‘unfair’ interference in my job. I support her in her jobs and passions, she should support me in this area. Yes, it is an unusual job, but it is still a job and pays the bills (plus free rent for a few years!).

But recently during one of our interchanges, I realized there was one critical flaw in my logic. I wasn’t talking about one job: a job I do and then go home to the family. It may not have always been true, but the modern presidency is two jobs. President and First Partner (so far ‘Lady’). Whether Rebecca would be paid as First Lady is irrelevant: she would clearly have to do the job. Maybe not legally, but it would look horrible for her not to do the job. And guilting someone into doing a job is as coercive as the many other means of force. It is unfair. I can’t make her be First Lady. She has to chose to be it (or ‘possibly it’) on her own.

Rebecca said no, so I am unfortunately ‘blocked’ on this path until her opinion changes. Seems unlikely to change any time soon, but I am in good enough physical condition to last for a few more election cycles.

Now What?

Unfortunately, this unusual path was a major part of my life, so shutting it down is a bit problematic. It is both constructively filling time for me and is enabling me to channel my energy and desire to ‘Do Good’. If I can’t (try to) be president, what can I do that would have as much impact? Or more?
