Ask the People

Jan 20th, 2015

I do not know the nuances of being a US President, but I have been CEO/President of multiple companies, and have been the technical lieutenant to a half-dozen CEO/Presidents. An important aspect of any job is to determine whether you are a follower or not: whether you do what someone else says. Note this has nothing to do with being a leader or not. If I transform the strategy of a game company into building the right software platform for the games, I have to lead a number of engineers to make that happen. The only time I am not a leader is when I don’t have any reports. And in that case I still need to lead myself :–)

Many people think otherwise, but not being a follower is not a fun thing. Being responsible for strategy where no one is leading you, being responsible for success where no one is giving you constructive (even critical) feedback, these are the terrors that can keep you up all night. I have experienced it as the CEO of Velidom. And on a few other occasions with much less at stake. Being a CEO is incredibly lonely because of this. You don’t have a partner, you don’t have a leader, you just have yourself. You are always to blame.

Fortunately, I think a US President has this one advantage: you must be a great leader (of hundreds of millions people), but you also have the benefit of needing to be a great follower (of hundreds of millions of people). You work for the people of the USA, and even the humanity of the whole world. Whenever you aren’t sure what you should do, just ask them. My script is “Imagine I was President of the USA. I work for you. What would you like me to do?” I even have an online version of this question at:

  • https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/InsanelyGreatPresident

As of a week in, I have asked this question to a couple dozen people. All of them were basically amazed by the concept. That makes me very sad, because that means people don’t feel they have a right (or all previous presidents don’t believe they have a right) to make this simple request. But it seems obvious to me that the person running a democracy should ask the people what they want him or her to do. Elections are majority rule, but that is just so some decision can be made. Elected presidents work for all the people not just the ones that elected them.

Among the couple dozen people, some were stymied. Almost “Oh my god! What a powerful question! Now I am in the hot seat!”. Others just needed a bit of time. Since I was asking people in my community, I would simply come back to them periodically to see if they were ready.

Interestingly, people understood the sincerity and power of the question and so far have not given trite answers. One girl said “My life is too good for me to make any requests”, which is incredibly adult if not useful of an answer. I will bug her some more later :–)

Other answers have included:

  • I would like more money to spend
  • I would like us to bring manufacturing back to the US
  • I would like us to be friendlier in the Middle East

The last answer was very interesting because the conversation up to the question was about economic difficulties living where we do. A moment earlier, we were talking about how this person really needed close to 3x the income to make ends meet. But when confronted with such a powerful question, the person switched to something more valuable than money.

Pride. In their country and its actions.

Now and Then

Jan 19th, 2015

Two critical aspects of any job are (a) Understanding what is required to submit your application to the proper people and (b) Understanding how great people have done the job before. Basically “Now” and “Then” where “Then” could include the person currently in the position.

Now

For the Now, you have to understand what is required by the FEC and to be put on the ballot in the fifty states. The basic information for all of this is at http://fec.gov

Then

For the Then, you have to decide what presidents inspire you and the people of the USA. There is an advantage of studying current presidents in that the political world is similar to what it will be for you. There is an advantage of studying earlier presidents in that enough time has passed to more truly evaluate them. Plus their are more of them to pick and chose from than from the last two decades.

So we have (a) current or (b) earlier. When given an apparent choice like that I have one common answer: “Yes!” There is no exclusion, just two options. Picking (a) or (b) exclusively would make you unbalanced and likely under-informed in a critical concept to the presidency. So now that we have the full set of options available, how do we prioritize which things to read first? Well, at least one consideration has to be which materials match your time and abilities to read. In my case, I am very commonly in my car going somewhere. So I can’t read a book safely. I need books on tape. And not all books are on tape. My second common situation is being in my car waiting for someone. At that point I can watch DVDs and read books. Or even do both. But again, I have to acquire the books and some of them are harder than others.

So what happened to me based on availability and diversity is that I am starting with four core biographies

  • Primary Colors (Audio)
  • FDR (Video)
  • Steve Jobs (Book)
  • Reagan (Book) : For this one I have a couple books and I haven’t decided which yet.

I have also acquired about a dozen more books and even started on “Team of Rivals”, but the details in the first half didn’t seem to be as pertinent to modern times as the other books, so I shifted it back into the main stack.

If I can through a book a week, I will be able to read fifty books a year. At worse, it would be closer to thirty.

The Start

Jan 18th, 2015

I am planning on competing at the ATP tournament in Indian Wells in March. As part of preparing for that tournament, I drove the sixteen-hour round trip in one day. That was the first day I didn’t play tennis (starting at 5AM) for about a year. Every day, rain or shine, cold or hot, I go out to the court in appropriate attire and practice. It is both athletic and meditative.

The day after the Indian Wells trip, I went out for my normal ritual. It is dark at 5AM, and the tennis court lights can’t be turned on until 6:03AM. After doing some of my practice, I sat there on the court. I must have pondered winning Indian Wells and making a large some of money off that. If I won, I could make quite a bit of money per year. Playing tennis and being sponsored. I already make quite a bit of money, so the obvious question to myself was “why?”. Winning Indian Wells would be notable because I am pretty old: will be 48 years old at the tournament and will turn 49 the month after. So there might be some karmic benefit or interesting learning from winning that one tournament. I was also planning on trying to win the US Open. That would be a real one-two punch to the youngsters.

But beyond the one or two punch combo… why? Why would I play tennis for money? What would it mean to the world? How would I be impacting the world, and why would I prefer that to my other choices?

I have a number of ways to make money, and I give away a lot of my time to help people (mostly kids). This is fine, but if I am going to change, it should be something better. Ideally much better. Much more impact. Positive impact.

I ponder some more and consider my characteristics and abilities. I care about people deeply. I care about everyone and not some specific group of people that matches some of the ideals I have. I solve problems. I am an execution expert and can deliver all kinds of great solutions to solve other people’s needs (or my own).

Where would an execution expert who cares deeply about the needs of everyone have the most positive impact?

Click. There is one job that fills that bill exactly. President. President of a country. President of my country. POTUS. President of the United States of America.

That is what I should try to be. I could have great positive impact in the role. I would work for hundreds of millions of people to protect and to serve them. What do these people need? What problems do they have? What would be the best solution? And how would I deliver that solution?

That is an awesome job. And I may be insanely great at doing it. But even if I am not elected, I can write about what I think (and experience) an insanely great president would do. To prepare, to get elected, and to serve the people of the USA when elected.
