Synopsis

About two years before the 2016 elections and the various candidates for the Republican, Democratic, and other parties start putting their hats in to the race. Most are unelectable, they have platforms that simply will not win their party, or if it did, it would not win the general election. But they decide to go in or out not because they are or want to be a great president, but for other political and personal reasons.

During this same season, on a dark tennis court at 5AM, a highly committed, educated, and unusually talented person sits to take a break from his morning routine. This is no politician: he graduated from Caltech, is a software and startup expert, and makes large sums of money helping protect Apple and others from their enemies. At forty eight he is preparing to challenge Rafeal Nadal and Roger Federrer. A lot of people call him insane, but strangely his insanity generally works, and helps the community in all kinds of interesting ways.

But making money has become meaningless, and beating Federrer would be interesting but not compelling. Not meaningful. “What would be meaningful?”

As his subconscious and conscious brain navigate through abilities and possibilities, this older, balding, white male in great intellectual and physical shape realizes only one job is perfect for him and he may be perfect for that job. That more than four decades of incredible focus and progress has turned him into the perfect executor. Or delivery boy. You tell him what you want, and he delivers it to you. What is the best possible delivery boy he could be?

And so a few minutes later he has his next career focus. He wants to be great at one job, and to try to get that one job. President. President of the United States of America. Not just any president, an Insanely Great President. A president better for the American people and even all of humanity than has been seen in decades.

This book will describe the preparation and the candidacy of a very different kind of person for one of the most important jobs on the planet. Whether he gets elected or not, it will show people a different kind of president, and how that Insanely Great President would be better at serving them than anyone they have ever elected before.

“Insanely Great President: To Serve and Protect” will be Mark’s second book. Where SOPA was a tale started thirty years in the past and spanned to current time, IGP will be “real-time”, and span from January 2015 when Mark decided to become President of the United States of America, through the elections in late 2016.

and has never voted: no candidate was truly impressive enough to vote for.

One morning at about 5AM, in the dark on a tennis court. Day after day he plays tennis alone in the dark and later in the morning light with others. Day after day he makes a fair amount of money as a software expert, and tries to unwind by 8PM so he can go to sleep. He is basically a border collie: ever eager to work and to please.

This particular morning he has just returned from the location of a major tennis tournament, sixteen hours round-trip away. Winning this tournament would be financially lucrative and would shock the world given he is forty eight years old. Losing the tournament is certainly likely, but failing doesn’t phase him. The winning part really phases him. “Why would I want to make money that way?”
